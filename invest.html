Step 1: Choose Your Platform

Selecting the right brokerage is your first decision

Your brokerage platform is where your investments will live. Consider factors like ease of use, investment options, fees, and customer service. Here are three popular, user-friendly options:

Brokerage Key Features Best For
Vanguard Low-cost index funds and ETFs, strong passive investing philosophy Long-term investors seeking low fees and broad market exposure
Fidelity Wide range of products, excellent research tools, good customer service All investor types, from beginners to advanced, seeking diverse options
Charles Schwab Commission-free trading, robust research, good for active and passive investors Investors seeking balance of features and low costs

Step 2: Decide on Account Type

Understanding your options

Joint Brokerage Account (Taxable Account)

Owned by both partners, allowing for shared contributions and management. Ideal for saving toward short-to-medium term goals like a down payment or large purchases, or for funds beyond retirement accounts. Profits are subject to capital gains tax.

Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs/Roth IRAs)

Owned by one individual, but contributions can be made by either spouse. These offer significant tax advantages for retirement savings. It's common for each spouse to have their own IRA or Roth IRA. Annual contribution limits apply.

For most couples starting out: A joint taxable brokerage account is the most straightforward way to pool resources for shared goals outside of retirement accounts.

Step 5: Automate It—The Secret to Consistency

Set it and forget it

Once your account is open and funded, set up recurring automatic transfers from your joint checking or savings account. This is arguably the most important step in the entire process. Automating your investments ensures consistency, removes emotion from the decision-making process, and allows you to benefit from dollar-cost averaging—the practice of investing a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of market conditions.

Even small, regular contributions add up significantly over time. A $500 monthly contribution invested consistently over 30 years at an average 7% annual return grows to over $800,000. Consistency is your superpower.

Pro Tip: Schedule your automatic transfer to occur a few days after your paycheck deposits. This ensures funds are available and removes the temptation to spend the money elsewhere.

What to Buy and Why

Simplified strategies for couples

The Power of Diversification

Deciding what to invest in can feel overwhelming. These cheat sheets provide simplified, effective strategies for couples, focusing on diversification and long-term growth. The goal is to create a diversified portfolio that minimizes risk while maximizing potential returns. For most couples, especially those starting out, simplicity is key.

We focus on broad market index funds and ETFs (Exchange Traded Funds) which offer instant diversification at low cost. You're essentially buying tiny pieces of thousands of companies, spreading your risk and increasing your chances of success.

Cheat Sheet 1: The "Set It & Forget It"

Target Date Funds

Best For: Beginners and hands-off investors

Target Date Funds (TDFs) are mutual funds that automatically adjust their asset allocation over time, becoming more conservative as you approach a specific retirement year. You simply choose the fund closest to your planned retirement date, and the fund does the rebalancing for you.

Asset Class Early Career (Aggressive) Mid-Career (Moderate) Near Retirement (Conservative)
Stocks 85-90% 70-80% 40-50%
Bonds 10-15% 20-30% 50-60%

Example: A 2050 Target Date Fund will hold more stocks now and gradually shift to more bonds as 2050 approaches, automatically managing your risk as you get closer to retirement.

How to Find It: Search your brokerage for "Target Date Fund" and look for one matching your planned retirement year (e.g., "Vanguard Target Retirement 2050 Fund").

Cheat Sheet 2: The "Simple 3-Fund Portfolio"

A bit more control, still simple

Best For: Couples who want control but prefer a straightforward approach

This strategy uses just three low-cost index funds to cover the entire global stock and bond markets. It's simple, diversified, and requires minimal maintenance.

The Three Funds:

1. Total US Stock Market Index Fund/ETF - Covers thousands of U.S. companies, from small startups to large corporations.

2. Total International Stock Market Index Fund/ETF - Covers thousands of non-U.S. companies, providing global diversification.

3. Total Bond Market Index Fund/ETF - Covers a broad range of U.S. investment-grade bonds, providing stability and income.

Risk Tolerance Total US Stock Total Intl Stock Total Bond
Aggressive 60% 30% 10%
Moderate 50% 25% 25%
Conservative 30% 20% 50%

Cheat Sheet 3: Risk Tolerance Matrix

Finding your comfort zone

Your combined risk tolerance is critical in determining your asset allocation. Discuss these questions together:

How would you react if your portfolio dropped by 20% in a single year? Would you panic and sell, or stay the course? Your answer reveals your true risk tolerance.

What are your investment time horizons for different goals? Longer time horizons allow for more stock exposure.

How comfortable are you with market fluctuations? Some couples sleep better with more stable, bond-heavy portfolios.

Risk Profile Description Stock/Bond Split
Aggressive Comfortable with significant volatility for higher returns; long time horizon 80-100% / 0-20%
Moderate Accept some volatility for growth; value capital preservation; medium time horizon 60-70% / 30-40%
Conservative Prioritize capital preservation; short-to-medium time horizon or nearing retirement 30-50% / 50-70%
